⚠️ Understanding Malware ⚠️

? What is Malware?

Malware, short for malicious software, is any program or file that is harmful to a computer user. It is designed to damage, disrupt, steal, or in general inflict some other bad or illegitimate action on systems, data, or networks.

🦠 Types of Malware

  • Viruses: 🦠 Attach to clean files and spread to other computers.
  • Worms: 🐛 Self-replicating malware that can spread without human interaction.
  • Trojans: 🐴 Disguise themselves as legitimate software to trick users into installing them.
  • Ransomware: 💰 Encrypts files and demands a ransom for their release.
  • Spyware: 👁️ Collects information about users without their knowledge.
  • Adware: 📢 Displays unwanted advertisements.

🌐 How Malware Spreads

  • Infected Websites: 🕸️ Visiting compromised websites.
  • Email Attachments: 📧 Opening malicious attachments.
  • Software Downloads: 💾 Downloading from untrusted sources.
  • Removable Drives: 💱 Infected USB drives or external hard drives.
  • Software Vulnerabilities: 🔓 Exploiting weaknesses in software.

🛡️ How to Protect Yourself

  • Antivirus Software: 🛡️ Install and keep updated antivirus software.
  • Firewall: 🔥 Enable and configure a firewall.
  • Software Updates: 🔄 Regularly update your operating system and software.
  • Be Cautious Online: 🤔 Be wary of suspicious links and downloads.
  • Strong Passwords: 🔑 Use strong, unique passwords.
  • Backups: 💾 Regularly back up your important data.
