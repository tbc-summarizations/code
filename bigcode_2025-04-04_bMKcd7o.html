John Coltrane

Biography

John William Coltrane (September 23, 1926 â€“ July 17, 1967), also known as "Trane", was an American jazz saxophonist and composer. Working in hard bop and modal jazz at the start of his career, Coltrane helped pioneer the use of modes in jazz and was later at the forefront of free jazz.

He organized at least fifty recording sessions as a leader and appeared on many albums by other musicians, most famously with Miles Davis and Thelonious Monk. Over the course of his career, Coltrane's music took on an increasingly spiritual dimension. His most famous works include "Giant Steps", "My Favorite Things", and "A Love Supreme".

Key Albums

  • Giant Steps (1960)

    A landmark album showcasing Coltrane's "Coltrane changes" and virtuosic playing.

  • My Favorite Things (1961)

    Features his soprano saxophone explorations and a popular rendition of the Rodgers and Hammerstein tune.

  • A Love Supreme (1965)

    A deeply spiritual and intensely personal four-part suite considered his masterpiece.

Influences and Legacy

Coltrane's influence on jazz music is immeasurable. He inspired generations of musicians across genres and continues to be studied and revered. His innovative techniques, harmonic explorations, and spiritual depth have solidified his place as one of the most important saxophonists in jazz history.
