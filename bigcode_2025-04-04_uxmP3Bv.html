Charlie Parker

Charles "Charlie" Parker Jr. (August 29, 1920 – March 12, 1955), also known as "Bird" and "Yardbird", was an American jazz saxophonist, band leader and composer. Parker was a highly influential soloist and leading figure in the development of bebop, a form of jazz characterized by fast tempos, virtuosic technique, and improvisation based on harmonic structure. Parker's innovative approaches to melody, harmony, and rhythm revolutionized jazz and influenced subsequent generations of musicians.

Key Facts

  • Born: August 29, 1920, Kansas City, KS
  • Died: March 12, 1955, New York City, NY
  • Genre: Jazz, Bebop
  • Instrument: Saxophone
  • Nickname: Bird, Yardbird

Parker's music is characterized by complex harmonies, intricate melodies, and a blues-infused improvisational style. He is considered one of the greatest and most influential jazz musicians of all time.
